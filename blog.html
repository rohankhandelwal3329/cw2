Why I Chose Computer Science

Posted: January 15, 2026  •  Reading Time: 5 minutes

When I first encountered programming in high school, I didn't immediately fall in love with it. In fact, I found it frustrating—why wouldn't my code just work? But then something clicked. I realized that debugging wasn't a failure; it was part of the journey. Each error message was a clue, each bug an opportunity to learn.

Fast forward to today, and I'm pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at Georgia State University. The decision wasn't easy—I considered business, engineering, and even medicine. But ultimately, I chose CS because it offers something unique: the power to create.

"Computer science is no more about computers than astronomy is about telescopes."

— Edsger Dijkstra

What excites me most about this field is its endless potential. From building websites that connect people across the globe to developing AI systems that can diagnose diseases—the possibilities are limitless. And that's why I wake up every day excited to learn more.

Key Takeaways

  1. Don't be discouraged by initial difficulties—programming has a learning curve
  2. Find what aspect of CS excites you most (web dev, AI, security, etc.)
  3. Build projects! Theory is important, but practice is essential
  4. Join communities and learn from others
  5. Stay curious and never stop learning


5 Things I've Learned from Daily Content Creation

Posted: January 10, 2026  •  Reading Time: 4 minutes

Running a YouTube channel with over 15,000 subscribers and maintaining a 400+ day daily upload streak has taught me more than any course ever could. Here are the five most valuable lessons I've learned:

1. Consistency Beats Perfection ✓

In the beginning, I spent hours perfecting each video. Now I understand that done is better than perfect. Showing up every day, even with imperfect content, builds momentum and trust with your audience.

2. Feedback is a Gift ☆

Every comment—positive or negative—is an opportunity to improve. Learning to accept criticism gracefully has made me a better creator and a better person.

3. Time Management is Everything ♣

Balancing daily uploads with a full-time course load requires serious planning. I've become a master of scheduling, batch-recording, and prioritization.

4. Community Matters ♥

My subscribers aren't just numbers—they're real people who take time out of their day to watch my content. Building genuine connections with them has been incredibly rewarding.

5. Skills Are Transferable ♦

Video editing, storytelling, audience analysis, SEO optimization—these skills apply far beyond YouTube. They've made me a better communicator and marketer.

Getting Started with HTML: A Beginner's Guide

Posted: January 5, 2026  •  Reading Time: 6 minutes

HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is the foundation of every website on the internet. If you're just starting your web development journey, HTML is the perfect place to begin. Here's what you need to know:

What is HTML?

HTML is a markup language that defines the structure and content of web pages. It uses "tags" to tell the browser how to display text, images, links, and other elements.

Essential HTML Tags to Know

Common HTML Tags and Their Uses
Tag Purpose Example
<h1> to <h6> Headings (h1 is largest) Page titles, section headers
<p> Paragraphs Body text, descriptions
<a> Hyperlinks Navigation, external links
<img> Images Photos, graphics, logos
<ul> / <ol> Lists Bullet points, numbered steps

Tips for Learning HTML

  • Practice daily — Build something small every day
  • Use semantic HTML — It improves accessibility and SEO
  • Validate your code — Use the W3C Validator to check for errors
  • View page source — Learn from websites you admire
  • Build real projects — Theory alone won't make you proficient
